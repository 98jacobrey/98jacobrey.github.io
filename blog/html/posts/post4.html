Debugging

The First Step to Refinement.

Posted on February 12, 2021

Programming is full of potential problems. These usually include improper data types, simple typos, faulty logic, or incorrect syntax. With these natural obstacles and much more standing in the way of programmers, a useful skill came in place. Debugging, the process of identifying and removing errors from computer software or hardware.

The tools at our disposal for diagnosing causes of bugs are plenty. From simple console log to setting up break statements for each individual function. Yet no tool can compare for a strong logical breakdown. Following these simple steps can help with the approach to all bugs. First, recognizing there is a bug and not ignoring the signs of something wrong within your project. Second, isolating the block of code that is failing to perform. Third, identifying the type and cause of the bug. Fourth, creating a solution to fix the problem. Fifth, apply the solution and test for results.

There are plenty of strategies out there and most of them can be explained with a simple Google search. That’s my secret ace in the hole. If you’re ever running into a problem, odds are you aren’t the only one. The internet has plenty of forums, blogs, and open source communities that usually hold the answer you seek. Simply finding which community suits you is the hardest part, but that also the most fun part about it.
